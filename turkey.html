Istanbul, Turkey

About Istanbul

Istanbul, where East meets West, is a city that bridges two continents—Europe and Asia. Its history spans thousands of years, with remnants of Byzantine, Roman, and Ottoman empires found throughout the city. Istanbul’s rich culture is reflected in its bustling bazaars, stunning mosques, and delicious Turkish cuisine. Visitors can enjoy sipping tea along the Bosphorus, exploring historic palaces, and soaking in the energy of this vibrant metropolis. Istanbul is also a shopper’s paradise, offering everything from handmade carpets to modern fashion.

Top Attractions

  • Hagia Sophia: A masterpiece of Byzantine architecture and a symbol of Istanbul.
  • Topkapi Palace: The opulent residence of Ottoman sultans.
  • Blue Mosque: A stunning mosque with intricate tilework and six minarets.
  • Grand Bazaar: One of the world’s oldest and largest covered markets.
  • Bosphorus Cruise: A scenic journey along Istanbul’s famous waterway.
  • Galata Tower: A medieval stone tower offering breathtaking city views.
